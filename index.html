24-Month People & Leadership Transformation Roadmap
Trip Factory: From No HR Infrastructure to Scalable Leadership Organisation

This 24-month roadmap focuses on two phases: Phase 1 — Protect & Stabilise (legal compliance, cost visibility, performance accountability, hiring governance) and Phase 2 — Strengthen & Scale (leadership depth, reward alignment, workforce analytics, Leadership Academy via ILM Level 5).

PHASE 1 — BUILD THE FOUNDATIONS Months 1–12
Q1–Q2 (Months 1–6) — Priority: Protect the Company
1. Legal & Compliance Stabilisation
  • Review all employment contracts globally
  • Correct contractor misclassification risks
  • Introduce core policies (Code of Conduct, Disciplinary, Grievance)
  • Standardise payroll governance
  • Ensure labour law compliance in all countries
Board Outcome: Reduced legal exposure • Clear governance framework • Lower employment risk
2. Basic HR Infrastructure
  • Central employee data system
  • Formal onboarding process
  • Standard offer letters and contracts
  • Clear organisational structure visibility
Board Outcome: Accurate headcount & workforce cost data • Clear reporting lines • Improved operational control
Q3–Q4 (Months 7–12) — Priority: Introduce Accountability & Cost Discipline
3. Workforce Mapping & Cost Transparency
  • Map all roles by function and geography
  • Identify duplication and capability gaps
  • Analyse workforce cost as % of revenue
Board Outcome: Workforce cost visibility • Identification of inefficiencies
4. Performance Management System
  • Introduce KPI-linked performance reviews
  • Define underperformance processes
  • Establish probation reviews
  • Link objectives to commercial outcomes
Board Outcome: Manager accountability • Clear productivity expectations
5. Recruitment Governance
  • Standard hiring approval framework
  • Structured interview methodology
  • Quality-of-hire metrics
Board Outcome: Reduced hiring risk • Better talent quality
End of Phase 1 — What the Board Should See
Fully compliant multi-country HR governance
Reliable workforce data
Controlled hiring processes
Performance accountability introduced
Reduced employment risk
Clear cost baseline established

Theme: Productivity, Internal Leadership Pipeline, Strategic Workforce Planning

1. Leadership & Succession Framework
  • Identify critical roles & map leadership bench strength

  • Introduce executive coaching standards

  • Introduce structured pay bands

Workflow 2 — Hotel Contracting Process
Side-by-side review: Actual execution (Singapore DMC) vs. Suggested framework
Actual Singapore DMC — As Executed
Suggested Recommended Standard Framework
1
Demand Intelligence Extraction
Contracting begins with system-driven demand validation. Reviewed: quote volume trends, Singapore pipeline ranking, forward travel calendar, itinerary patterns, and group movement indicators. No contracting without demand-backed volume visibility.
2
Demand-to-Supply Translation
Quote activity converted into projected room-night requirement across forward travel windows. Segmented: Leisure FIT (primary), Incentive Groups (mid-range), Corporate Groups (premium). Supply requirement quantified before any hotel engagement.
3
Cluster Prioritisation & Hotel Targeting
System data identifies primary cluster (Little India – volume) and premium clusters (Orchard / Marina / Sentosa). Top quoted and high-conversion properties extracted. Creates three-tier list: Core Volume, Premium Yield, Group-Compatible. "We contract system-relevant hotels, not nice hotels."
4
Strategic Supplier Engagement
Chain-level partnerships leveraged (Accor, Millennium). Direct GM/DOS approach where required. Formal proposal submitted: 300-day demand plan, utilisation forecast, buying rate framework. Positioned as volume partner — not rate requester.
5
Commercial Negotiation
Structured around: static net buying rate, validity alignment, release period, group policy, volume commitment balance. All discussions backed by live demand data. Negotiation = Rate + Volume + Risk Sharing — not price-only.
6
Contract Finalisation
Agreement executed, commitment documented, commercial controls validated. No informal rate activation. No verbal rate usage. Governance enforced.
7
System Integration & Activation
Rate normalisation → margin validation → inventory upload → QA → activation across B2B & B2C. Commercial control before distribution scale.
Operating Principles
Demand-first • Volume-structured negotiation • Cluster-prioritised supply build • Commercially controlled activation • System-integrated distribution
1
Hotel Targeting & Prioritisation
Hotels shortlisted by category (3★/4★/5★), location within destination, and past sales performance. Minimum inventory requirements assessed.
Gap: Actual model starts with demand data first — hotel targeting is an output of demand analysis, not the entry point.
2
Initial Negotiation & Terms Discussion

Aligned: Actual model uses live demand data to validate commercial terms throughout.
4
Counter-Negotiation

Key Strength — Actual Model
The Singapore execution model is demand-first and data-driven — more advanced than the suggested framework. It should become the Trip Factory standard for all destination contracting.
Key Gap — To Close
The suggested framework adds structure around content loading, QA, and system activation. Merging both creates a complete, governed contracting framework for all destinations.
Workflow 3 — Rate Loading & System Upload
1
Rate Sheet Receipt & Validation
Contracted rate sheet received from supplier (hotel, ground operator, or DMC). Validated against signed contract terms — dates, inclusions, room categories, blackouts. Any discrepancy flagged back to supplier before upload. Output: Validated rate sheet.
2
Rate Formatting & Standardisation
Rate sheet converted to Trip Factory's standard upload template (CSV/Excel format defined by Tech). Currencies confirmed, meal plan codes mapped, and seasonal date bands set correctly. Output: Upload-ready rate file.
3
Margin & Markup Application
Net rates loaded with Trip Factory's margin model applied (destination-specific markup percentages). Sell rates cross-checked against market pricing benchmarks. Approved by Contracting Manager or VP Product. Output: Sell-rate schedule.
4
System Upload & QA
Rates uploaded to platform by Product Executives. Post-upload QA: test booking created for each key date range, room type, and occupancy. Rates checked on B2C and B2B-facing views. Output: QA sign-off log.
5
Rate Availability & Go-Live Confirmation
Contracting team confirms rates live with Sales, B2C, and B2B team leads. Any rate corrections resolved within 24 hours of go-live. Supplier notified of live status. Output: Rate go-live notification.
6
Rate Monitoring & Update Cycle
Live rates monitored for parity against OTAs (monthly spot-check). Supplier amendments and rate extensions processed within the same workflow. Annual re-contracting cycle initiated 60 days before contract expiry.
Workflow 4 — Market Comparison & Competitive Benchmarking
1
Benchmark Source Identification
Contracting team identifies primary comparison sources: OTA published rates (Booking.com, Expedia, Agoda), competitor DMC price lists (where accessible), and mystery shop of competitor B2B portals.
2
Rate Extraction & Compilation
Spot rates extracted for target properties across key travel dates. Compiled into a destination-level comparison matrix (property vs. channel vs. Trip Factory net/sell rate). Output: Monthly comparison report.
3
Gap Analysis & Flagging
Properties where Trip Factory's sell rate is significantly above or below market flagged for action. Over-priced: re-negotiate with supplier or adjust margin. Under-priced: opportunity to improve margin or review against contract terms.
4
Negotiation Trigger
Where gap analysis shows Trip Factory is at a competitive disadvantage, Contracting Manager initiates re-negotiation with supplier. Supporting data from comparison matrix used as leverage.
5
Reporting to Leadership
Monthly competitive benchmarking summary presented to VP Product and relevant Sales leads. Key wins (new rate advantages) and risks (parity breaches) highlighted. Feeds into annual contracting strategy.
Workflow 5 — Ground Operator & DMC Contracting
1
Ground Operator Identification
For each destination, DMC or ground operator shortlisted based on: existing network, referrals from operational team, trade associations, and competitor intelligence. Minimum 2 operators per destination for redundancy.
2
Due Diligence & Qualification
Operator evaluated on: financial stability, insurance cover, vehicle fleet quality, local licencing, and references. Site visit or video call conducted for new operators. Output: Operator qualification scorecard.
3
Service & Rate Negotiation
Transfers, sightseeing, guides, and assistance services negotiated. Per-pax and per-vehicle rates agreed for various group sizes. Seasonal surcharges, peak supplements, and FOC ratios negotiated.
4
Contract Execution
Service agreement covering rates, cancellation terms, liability, insurance requirements, and payment cycle. Countersigned by both parties. Filed with Contracting and Finance. Output: Executed ground operator agreement.
5
System Integration & Package Build
Ground services loaded into platform linked to destination and hotel packages. Product Executives build standard transfer and sightseeing combinations. Packages made available to B2C and B2B teams for quoting.
6
Operator Performance Review
Quarterly review of operator performance: on-time rate, complaint resolution, and Operations team feedback. Persistent issues trigger re-negotiation or supplier switch. Annual contract renewal based on performance score.
Strategic Recommendations

Key priorities for the Contracting function: implement a contract management system (Salesforce, Zoho, or dedicated hotel contracting tools) to replace spreadsheet-based tracking; enforce a standard 90-day pre-expiry re-negotiation trigger for all contracts; build a centralised rate parity dashboard that auto-flags OTA breaches; and formalise the new destination onboarding checklist to reduce time from decision to live rates.
